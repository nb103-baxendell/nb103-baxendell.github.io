Human-Computer Interaction Case Study

Prototyping the mobile application "Blue on the Move" with Megan Yi and Gwen-Zoe Yang

Project Overview

The culture surrounding participation in athletics at Wellesley often limits students’ options to either physical education classes, varsity sports, or a limited number of high-commitment club sports. Additionally, there are few centralized modes of communication, online or offline, for students to talk about and create networks for recreational sports. Through development of the mobile application “Blue on the Move,” we hope to address this by providing a means for students to find and schedule group recreational sports activities, join and communicate with recreational sports groups that match their interests, and find individual app users with similar athletic goals. Through an iterative process involving contextual inquiry interviews, prototype development, and usability testing, we aim to build a final prototype that will improve physical and social health of students at Wellesley College.

Mobile App Objectives

  • Provide all-in-one functionality for participating in recreational sports at Wellesley.
  • Facilitate individual and group social connection through group and personal profiles and chats
  • Offer a streamlined, clean, and learnable design for users by relying on design principles and formats similar to other social media

Methodology

  1. Needfinding analysis and persona development
  2. Contextual inquiry interviews
  3. Low-fidelity prototyping via paper sketches, storyboards, and a Figma prototype
  4. Usability testing for low-fidelity Figma prototype on 3 users matching key personas
  5. High-fidelity prototyping on Figma using insights from usability testing
  6. Usability testing evaluation on high-fidelity prototype on 6 users
  7. Presentation of process and results

My Contributions

  • At weekly team meeting, provided action items for each team member to meet our weekly to bi-weekly milestone deadlines.
  • Conducted 1 solo contextual interview and led or took notes for all high-fidelity usability testing
  • Contributed to the design of low-fidelity and high-fidelity Figma prototypes, with key contributions to prototyping event-related screens and user & group profiles.
