A sample of my projects!

2025 Summer Research Poster Presentation

Continuing my work with geoscience advisor Adrian Castro, I created and presented this poster summarizing 9 weeks of full-time research.

My research focused on methods of petrography, thermodynamic modeling, and geochemical instrumentation and analysis to uncover the tectonic history of the Manhattan Prong. Specifically, I focused on applying phase equilibria techniques to unravel the dynamics of melt segregation and loss during anatexis.

Independent Study Spring 2025 Ruhlman Presentation

Along with collaborator Riley Weed and advisor Adrian Castro, created and presented a poster at the 2025 Ruhlman Conference at Wellesley College. Poster summarized and communicated for a general scientific audience 1 semester of research on the pressure - temperature - deformation conditions of Paleozoic metamorphism on the rocks of modern-day Manhattan.

2024 Summer Research Poster Session Presentation

As a researcher in the Monecke Lab at Wellesley College during summer 2024, myself and collaborators Grace Knuth and Mingrui Ma created this poster communicating two months of full-time research on the sediment depositional history of Massachusett's famous Walden Pond.

This poster was presented at a conference of undergraduate resarchers at Wellesley, and developed my scientific communication skills as well as representing the technical research skills in the geological sciences I had developed over this project.

CAMS 100 Video Essay: "The Poor Image Chainletter: The Poor Image as Cultural Capital Within Queer and Feminist Media Movements"

For my final project in CAMS 100: Introduction to Media and Screen Cultures, I explored filmmaker Hito Steyerl's concept of the "poor image," or low-quality, accessible media through the lens of feminist and queer media distribution circuits and archives.

This project culminated in a paper and short video essay using the concepts of cinematography and media design learned in class.
